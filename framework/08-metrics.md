# Metrics: MIK and the Pirate Metrics

If you can't measure it, you can't improve it. But measuring everything is just as bad as measuring nothing. The key is choosing the *right* metric for your stage and making it the single source of truth.

## The Most Important KPI (MIK)

Your MIK is the one number that tells you whether you're making progress. Everything else is either a supporting metric (for diagnosis) or a vanity metric (for your ego).

Having one guiding KPI eliminates waffling, conflicting priorities, and work that doesn't move the needle.

### Choosing Your MIK by Stage

| Stage | Good MIK Options | Why |
|-------|-----------------|-----|
| Idea | # of customer interviews completed | You need learning velocity |
| Problem/Solution Fit | # of "delighted" customers (would be very disappointed without you) | You need to prove the problem and solution matter |
| Customer/Solution Fit | Monthly active users or MRR (if charging) | You need repeatable value delivery |
| Product/Market Fit | MRR or monthly growth rate | You need scalable, repeatable revenue |

### What's NOT a Good MIK

- **Followers/likes/impressions** — vanity metrics that feel good but don't correlate with business success
- **Lines of code / features shipped** — output metrics that measure effort, not value
- **"Conversations had"** — activity metrics with no quality signal
- **Multiple KPIs** — if everything is important, nothing is

## Complementary Metrics: AARRR (Pirate Metrics)

While your MIK gives you focus, you need a few supporting metrics for diagnosis. Dave McClure's Pirate Metrics cover the full customer journey:

| Metric | Question | Example |
|--------|----------|---------|
| **Acquisition** | How do people find you? | Website visits, sign-ups, app downloads |
| **Activation** | Do they have a good first experience? | Completed onboarding, first key action |
| **Retention** | Do they come back? | DAU/MAU, d7/d30 retention |
| **Revenue** | Do they pay you? | MRR, conversion to paid, ARPU |
| **Referral** | Do they tell others? | NPS, referral rate, word-of-mouth sign-ups |

Track all five, but only optimize for what's stage-appropriate.

## What NOT to Track Yet

If you're pre-revenue or pre-PMF, don't waste time on:
- **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)** — irrelevant until you have a repeatable channel
- **LTV (Lifetime Value)** — you don't have enough data yet
- **Churn rate** — meaningful only with enough customers over enough time

These become critical later. For now, focus on getting those first 10 delighted customers.

## Experiment Metrics vs. Business Metrics

Your MIK and AARRR metrics track overall business progress. Your *experiment* metrics are different — they measure the success or failure of a specific test.

Example: Your MIK might be "number of paying customers." But a specific experiment to test your pricing page might measure "conversion rate from pricing page visit to checkout." The experiment metric serves the MIK, but they're not the same thing.
