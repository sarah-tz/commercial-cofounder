# The Commercial Cofounder Process

## The Core Loop

Everything in this framework serves one purpose: **reduce uncertainty about your business by testing assumptions as fast as possible.**

```
  ┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │                                         │
  ▼                                         │
BELIEVE → IDENTIFY → PRIORITIZE → TEST → LEARN
  │                                         ▲
  │                                         │
  └─────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

You start with beliefs about your customer, your problem, and your solution. You make those beliefs explicit (assumptions). You figure out which ones are riskiest. You test the riskiest ones first. You learn. You update your beliefs. Repeat.

The faster you move through this loop, the faster you learn. The faster you learn, the more likely you are to build something people actually want.

## The Process Steps

| # | Step | What You Do | Output |
|---|------|------------|--------|
| 1 | **W3 Questions** | Deep dive on Who your customer is, What they're buying, Why they buy | Specific ICP + JTBD clarity |
| 2 | **LEAN Canvas** | Map your entire business model on one page | Business model hypothesis |
| 3 | **Customer Insights** | Organize what you know/believe about customer Pains, Gains, JTBD | Structured customer map |
| 4 | **Assumptions** | Extract every belief, categorize by Desirability/Feasibility/Viability, prioritize | Ranked assumption list |
| 5 | **Value Proposition** | Articulate what you do in a way that makes people get it instantly | Clear, testable value prop |
| 6 | **MSC + Sizing** | Define minimum success + back-of-envelope math on what it takes | Reality check on your model |
| 7 | **Goals & Milestones** | Set clear targets with intermediate checkpoints | Actionable goal structure |
| 8 | **Metrics** | Choose your Most Important KPI (MIK) + supporting AARRR metrics | Measurement framework |
| 9 | **Experimentation** | Design and run tests using Test Cards and Learning Cards | Validated/invalidated assumptions |
| 10 | **Weekly Review** | Track progress, update metrics, decide next experiments | Continuous momentum |

## Startup Stages

Where you are determines what you should focus on. Don't optimize for metrics that don't matter at your stage.

| Stage | You're Here If... | Focus On | # Paid Customers |
|-------|-------------------|----------|-----------------|
| **Pre-Idea** | You don't have a specific problem yet | Discovering a real problem worth solving | 0 |
| **Idea** | You have a problem hypothesis but no validation | Validating the problem exists and matters | 0 |
| **Problem/Solution Fit** | Problem is validated, testing solutions | Getting 10 delighted customers with a high-touch offer | 0-10 |
| **Customer/Solution Fit** | Solution works, proving it's repeatable | MVP that meets JTBD, creating delighted customers repeatedly | 10-100 |
| **Product/Market Fit** | Repeatable value, finding scalable channels | Repeatable sales, positive unit economics | 100-1,000 |
| **Scale** | PMF achieved, removing founder bottlenecks | Building operations, unlocking growth by multiples | 1,000-10,000 |

Most founders using this framework will be in the **Idea** through **Customer/Solution Fit** stages. That's where the framework is most useful.

## Key Frameworks Referenced

- **Lean Startup** (Eric Ries) — Build-Measure-Learn loop, MVP thinking
- **Jobs-to-be-Done** (Christensen/Ulwick) — Customers hire solutions to get jobs done
- **Lean Canvas** (Ash Maurya) — Business model on a page
- **Value Proposition Design** (Strategyzer/Osterwalder) — Customer map + value map
- **Test/Learning Cards** (Strategyzer) — Structured experiment design and capture
- **Pirate Metrics AARRR** (Dave McClure) — Acquisition, Activation, Retention, Revenue, Referral

## The One Rule

**Focus.** One customer segment. One product. One value proposition. You don't have the resources to do more than one of each right now. Pick the most promising one and go all in. You can always come back to the others later.
