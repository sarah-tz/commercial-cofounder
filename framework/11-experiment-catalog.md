# Experiment Catalog: 20 Scrappy GTM Tests

Before you build anything, test your assumptions with the cheapest possible experiment. This catalog gives you options organized by what you're trying to learn, with time and evidence-strength ratings.

Pick the experiment that matches your riskiest assumption. If you're not sure what to test, run `/assumptions` first.

---

## 1. Signal Mining, Discovery & Market Sizing

These experiments help you answer: *Is there a market here? How big? What language do people use?*

### Search Volume Analysis
Mine search data to see if people are actively looking for solutions to the problem you're solving.

- Check Google Trends, Keyword Planner, and SEMrush/Ahrefs for relevant search terms
- Compare volume across problem-related vs. solution-related keywords
- Look for trend direction — growing, flat, or declining

**Ingredients:** Access to keyword tools (free tiers work), list of problem/solution terms
**Setup time:** 1 hour | **Run time:** 2-3 hours
**Evidence strength:** 2/5

### Roundtable
Host a small group discussion with 4-6 people in your target segment to surface shared pains and language.

- Recruit participants via LinkedIn, communities, or warm intros
- Prepare 3-5 open-ended questions about their current workflow and frustrations
- Record (with permission) and note recurring themes and exact phrases

**Ingredients:** 4-6 participants in your ICP, video call tool, 60-90 minutes of their time
**Setup time:** 1 week (recruiting) | **Run time:** 90 minutes
**Evidence strength:** 2/5

### Partner Interviews
Talk to people who already serve your target customer — agencies, consultants, resellers — to understand the ecosystem.

- Identify 5-10 partners/adjacent service providers who work with your ICP
- Ask: What do their clients complain about? What tools do they wish existed? What's broken?
- Map the buying process and decision-makers they interact with

**Ingredients:** List of adjacent service providers, LinkedIn or email for outreach
**Setup time:** 3-5 days | **Run time:** 1-2 weeks
**Evidence strength:** 2/5

### Forum Audit
Systematically scan online communities where your target customers hang out to find unmet needs.

- Identify 3-5 relevant forums, subreddits, Slack/Discord communities, or Facebook groups
- Search for complaint patterns, workaround descriptions, and "I wish..." statements
- Document frequency of pain mentions and exact language used

**Ingredients:** List of communities, spreadsheet for tracking patterns
**Setup time:** 1 hour | **Run time:** 4-8 hours
**Evidence strength:** 2/5

### Review Mining
Analyze reviews of competing or adjacent products to find gaps and unmet needs.

- Pull reviews from G2, Capterra, App Store, Amazon, or Trustpilot for 3-5 competitors
- Focus on 2-3 star reviews (specific complaints) and filter for your ICP
- Categorize complaints into themes and note frequency

**Ingredients:** Competitor list, review platforms, spreadsheet
**Setup time:** 30 minutes | **Run time:** 4-6 hours
**Evidence strength:** 2/5

## 2. Demand Validation

These experiments help you answer: *Will people actually want this? Will they pay? Will they use it?*

### Mom Test Interviews
Have honest conversations with potential customers to validate (or kill) your problem and solution assumptions. Named after Rob Fitzpatrick's rule: even your mom should be able to give you useful feedback if you ask the right questions.

- Talk about their life, not your idea. Ask about specific past behavior, not hypotheticals
- Never tell them what your idea is until after you've learned what you need
- Ask for commitments or advancement, not compliments

**Ingredients:** 10-15 potential customers, list of assumptions to test, 30 min per conversation
**Setup time:** 3-5 days (recruiting) | **Run time:** 2-3 weeks
**Evidence strength:** 3/5

### Webinar / Workshop
Teach something valuable related to the problem you solve. Attendance and engagement signal demand.

- Pick a topic that sits at the intersection of their pain and your expertise
- Promote via the channels your ICP uses (LinkedIn, communities, email)
- Measure: signups, show-up rate, engagement during Q&A, post-event interest in "learning more"

**Ingredients:** Slide deck, webinar tool, promotion channel, landing page
**Setup time:** 1 week | **Run time:** 1-2 hours (plus follow-up)
**Evidence strength:** 3/5

### POC / Proof of Concept Test
Offer to solve the problem manually for 2-3 customers to validate willingness to pay and workflow fit.

- Find 2-3 target customers willing to let you solve their problem (even manually)
- Deliver the outcome using whatever tools you have — spreadsheets, manual work, existing software
- Measure: Did they get value? Would they pay for this ongoing? What would they pay?

**Ingredients:** 2-3 willing participants, your time and expertise, way to deliver the outcome
**Setup time:** 1-2 weeks | **Run time:** 2-4 weeks
**Evidence strength:** 4/5

### Email Test Sale
Email potential customers with a specific offer and see if they bite. Real buying intent, not just interest.

- Write a clear, specific offer email — what you'll do, for whom, by when, at what price
- Send to 20-50 warm or targeted prospects
- Measure: reply rate, "tell me more" rate, actual conversion to paid/committed

**Ingredients:** Email list of 20-50 prospects, clear offer, way to fulfill if they say yes
**Setup time:** 2-3 days | **Run time:** 1-2 weeks
**Evidence strength:** 3/5

### Empty Box Test
Sell the product before it exists. Put up a real purchase flow and measure who tries to buy.

- Create a product listing, pricing page, or sales page with a buy/signup button
- When they click "buy," show a "coming soon" message, waitlist, or pre-order confirmation
- Measure: visit-to-click rate, email captures, pre-orders

**Ingredients:** Landing page, payment or waitlist tool, traffic source
**Setup time:** 1-2 days | **Run time:** 1-2 weeks
**Evidence strength:** 4/5

### False Door Test
Add a feature button or menu item that doesn't exist yet. Measure how many people click it.

- Add a UI element for the feature you're considering (button, menu item, link)
- When clicked, show "Coming soon — want early access?" with an email capture
- Measure: click rate relative to page views, email signups

**Ingredients:** Existing product or website with traffic, ability to add UI elements
**Setup time:** 2-4 hours | **Run time:** 1-2 weeks
**Evidence strength:** 3/5

### Concierge / Wizard of Oz
Deliver the product experience manually behind the scenes while the customer thinks it's automated.

- Build a front-end that looks like a real product (landing page, form, dashboard)
- When a customer uses it, you manually do the work behind the scenes
- Measure: customer satisfaction, willingness to pay, where the process breaks

**Ingredients:** Front-end (even a simple form), your time to manually deliver, 5-10 customers
**Setup time:** 1-2 weeks | **Run time:** 2-4 weeks
**Evidence strength:** 4/5

### Crowdfunding Campaign
Launch on Kickstarter, Indiegogo, or similar to test demand with real money on the table.

- Create a campaign page with clear value prop, pricing tiers, and delivery timeline
- Set a funding goal that represents your minimum viable demand
- Measure: funded or not, backer count, average pledge, which tier is most popular

**Ingredients:** Campaign page, video (even simple), reward tiers, promotion plan
**Setup time:** 2-4 weeks | **Run time:** 30-60 days
**Evidence strength:** 5/5

### Podcast MVT (Minimum Viable Test)
Appear on or create a podcast episode about the problem you solve. Gauge audience reaction and inbound interest.

- Find 3-5 podcasts your ICP listens to, or record a short episode yourself
- Discuss the problem (not your product) and offer a specific CTA (free resource, waitlist, consultation)
- Measure: inbound interest, CTA conversions, quality of conversations that follow

**Ingredients:** Podcast list or recording setup, topic outline, CTA with tracking
**Setup time:** 1-2 weeks | **Run time:** 2-4 weeks (for booking + airing)
**Evidence strength:** 2/5

## 3. Positioning & GTM Prep

These experiments help you answer: *How should we describe this? What messaging lands? Which channel works?*

### Survey
Send a structured questionnaire to your target segment to quantify preferences, priorities, and willingness to pay.

- Design 8-12 questions max — mix of multiple choice, ranking, and one open-ended
- Distribute via email, social, or communities where your ICP hangs out
- Aim for 50+ responses minimum for any quantitative conclusions

**Ingredients:** Survey tool (Typeform, Google Forms), distribution channel, 50+ respondents
**Setup time:** 2-3 days | **Run time:** 1-2 weeks
**Evidence strength:** 2/5

### Five-Second Test
Show your landing page, ad, or headline to people for 5 seconds. Can they tell you what it does and who it's for?

- Create 2-3 versions of your headline/hero section
- Show each version to 10-20 people for exactly 5 seconds, then hide it
- Ask: "What does this product do?" and "Who is it for?" — measure comprehension accuracy

**Ingredients:** 2-3 messaging variants, UsabilityHub or manual testing, 20-40 testers
**Setup time:** 2-3 hours | **Run time:** 1-3 days
**Evidence strength:** 3/5

### Meta Ad Test
Run cheap Facebook/Instagram ads with different value props, headlines, or audiences to see what resonates.

- Create 3-5 ad variants testing different messages, pain points, or audiences
- Run each with a small budget ($5-20/day per variant) for 3-7 days
- Measure: CTR (click-through rate) as a proxy for message resonance, not conversions

**Ingredients:** Ad account, $50-200 budget, landing page or lead form, 3-5 ad variants
**Setup time:** 1-2 days | **Run time:** 3-7 days
**Evidence strength:** 3/5

### Landing Page Test
Put up a landing page describing your product and drive traffic to it. Measure interest via signups, clicks, or waitlist joins.

- Build a focused page: headline, 3 benefits, social proof if you have it, one CTA
- Drive traffic via ads, social posts, communities, or cold outreach
- Measure: visit-to-signup conversion rate, time on page, scroll depth

**Ingredients:** Landing page builder, traffic source ($50-200 in ads or organic distribution), analytics
**Setup time:** 1-2 days | **Run time:** 1-2 weeks
**Evidence strength:** 3/5

## 4. Pricing & Monetization

These experiments help you answer: *What should we charge? What's the market willing to pay?*

### Van Westendorp Price Sensitivity
Ask 4 simple questions to map the acceptable price range for your product.

- Ask each respondent: At what price would this be (1) too cheap to trust, (2) a bargain, (3) getting expensive, (4) too expensive?
- Plot the cumulative distributions to find the acceptable price range and optimal price point
- Need 30+ respondents from your actual ICP for usable data

**Ingredients:** 30+ ICP respondents, survey tool, clear product description
**Setup time:** 1-2 days | **Run time:** 1-2 weeks
**Evidence strength:** 3/5

### High-Ball Pricing
Start with a price that feels uncomfortably high and negotiate down. You'll learn the ceiling faster than anchoring low.

- Quote your highest defensible price in the first 3-5 sales conversations
- Track reactions: immediate yes (price too low), negotiation (you're in range), walkaway (found the ceiling)
- Adjust based on patterns, not individual reactions

**Ingredients:** 5-10 sales conversations, confidence to name a high number, tracking spreadsheet
**Setup time:** None | **Run time:** 1-3 weeks
**Evidence strength:** 4/5

## Evidence-Based vs. Faith-Based Decision Making

Most startups default to the faith-based approach. The experiment catalog exists to make evidence-based the default.

**Faith-based decisions:**
Opinion → Debate → More Opinions → Decision. Whoever argues loudest wins. Cadence: weeks per project. You feel productive because you're busy, but you're not learning.

**Evidence-based decisions:**
Hypothesis → Test → Data → Decision. What customers actually do beats what anyone thinks they'll do. Cadence: 3-10 tests per week. You feel uncertain because you're confronting reality, but you're actually making progress.

The goal isn't to run experiments for the sake of it. The goal is to make decisions based on what customers do, not what you hope they'll do. Every week without an experiment is a week where your loudest opinion is running the company.
