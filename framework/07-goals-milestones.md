# Goals and Milestones

Goals give you direction. Milestones tell you if you're making progress. Without both, you're just busy.

## Setting Your Goal

A good goal is:
- **Aligned** — moves you toward product-market fit
- **Achievable** — challenging but realistic in your timeframe
- **Impactful** — moves the needle on what matters most

Examples:
- "Validate a viable beachhead market for our offer"
- "Find repeatable sales channels for this market"
- "Get 10 paying customers who actively use the product weekly"

Anti-examples:
- "Build the product" (that's a task, not a goal)
- "Get funding" (that's an outcome, not something you directly control)
- "Grow the business" (too vague to act on)

## Setting Milestones

Milestones are the intermediate steps. You know you've reached one because it's specific and binary — either you hit it or you didn't.

Examples:
- "Complete assumption map and identify top 5 riskiest assumptions"
- "Conduct 15 customer discovery interviews"
- "Secure first 3 paying customers"
- "Achieve 40% week-1 retention in our beta"

## Assigning Actions

Each milestone needs concrete actions with clear ownership. Even if you're solo, write them down — it turns vague intent into commitment.

For each milestone:
1. What specific actions will get you there?
2. What's the deadline?
3. What does "done" look like?

## Review Cadence

Review your goals and milestones regularly:
- **Weekly:** Are you making progress? What's blocking you?
- **Monthly:** Are your milestones still the right ones? Has anything changed?
- **Quarterly:** Is your goal still the right goal? Do you need to pivot?
